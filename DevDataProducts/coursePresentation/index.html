1. Introduction

Looking for a new place to live can be a really hard, time consuming (and expensive!) adventure, especially if you move to a new city and you are still learning which district you will fit in. BostonRE is trying to solve this problem.

We are analyzing the relations between a set of measures and the house prices in the Boston area. Based on them, we build a predictive model that will estimate the price of a house for your input parameters.

The dataset for building this model is the BostonHousing2 from the mlbench library which contains 506 observations with 14 variables from the 1970 census.

2. Exploratory analysis

  • The number of rooms seems related with price (shape vs size)
  • Crime rate seems very correlated with location and price: We can identify clusters of data by color (crime rate).
  • We also observe that in high crime rate areas (purple), independently of the number of rooms the prices are lower than in other regions.
